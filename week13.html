Week 13

Overview

This week focused on upgrading the web application developed in Week 10 and integrating our trained change detection model into the backend for real-world testing. I enhanced the Flask application with improved model loading mechanisms, optimized inference pipeline, and added comprehensive testing capabilities. The upgraded interface now supports batch processing, confidence visualization, and detailed performance metrics. Extensive testing was conducted using both our collected Sri Lanka flood dataset and the newly acquired wildfire samples to validate model performance across different disaster types.

Detailed Update

Web Application Enhancements

Building on the initial web application framework from Week 10, this week involved significant upgrades to support production-ready deployment and comprehensive model testing.

Model Testing and Validation

Test Scenarios Conducted:

1. Flood Detection (Sri Lanka Dataset):

  • Tested on 30+ flood image pairs from various Sri Lankan locations
  • Locations tested: Kelani River basin, Mahaweli River, Negombo Lagoon, and other flood-prone areas
  • Disaster types: Urban flooding, riverine flooding, coastal flooding
  • Results:
    • Average IoU: ~0.74
    • Average F1-score: ~0.80
    • Strong performance on clear flooding scenarios
    • Excellent detection of water extent changes
    • Some challenges with partial flooding and wet soil differentiation
    • Good performance across different geographic regions

2. Landslide Detection (Sri Lanka Dataset):

  • Tested on 15+ landslide-affected locations from November 2024 disaster
  • Regions tested: Ratnapura, Kegalle, hill country areas
  • Change types: Landslide scars, vegetation loss, debris flows, infrastructure damage
  • Results:
    • Average IoU: ~0.70
    • Average F1-score: ~0.76
    • Successfully identifies large landslide scars and slope failures
    • Good detection of vegetation loss in affected areas
    • Challenges with small debris flows in dense forested areas
    • Some difficulty distinguishing landslides from natural bare soil in hilly terrain

3. Mixed Impact Areas:

  • Tested on locations affected by both floods and landslides
  • Results:
    • Model successfully detects both change types simultaneously
    • Combined disasters show higher change detection complexity
    • Overall performance remains robust with Average IoU: ~0.72

Performance Analysis:

  • Inference Speed:
    • Single prediction: ~2-3 seconds (including preprocessing)
    • Batch of 10: ~15 seconds (batched inference)
    • GPU utilization: ~70-80% during inference
  • Strengths Identified:
    • Excellent performance on water extent mapping (floods)
    • Strong detection of large-scale vegetation loss (landslides)
    • Robust to different lighting conditions and seasons
    • Good boundary delineation for large change regions
    • Handles Sri Lankan terrain variations well (coastal, riverine, mountainous)
    • Effective across different disaster types within the same region
  • Limitations Identified:
    • Occasional false positives in shadow areas (especially in mountainous terrain)
    • Difficulty with very small change regions (<50 pixels)
    • Some confusion between wet soil and shallow water in flood scenarios
    • Challenges with partial cloud cover despite preprocessing efforts
    • Small debris flows in dense vegetation can be missed

Testing Insights and Future Directions

Comprehensive testing on Sri Lankan flood and landslide scenarios revealed several important insights:

  • Dataset Quality Impact: Model performs best on high-quality, cloud-free images from our preprocessing pipeline
  • Transfer Learning Success: EuroSAT pre-training provides strong foundation for Sri Lankan terrain
  • Regional Adaptation: Model successfully adapted to diverse Sri Lankan geographic conditions
  • Multi-Disaster Capability: Single model handles both floods and landslides effectively
  • Fine-Tuning Opportunities: Location-specific fine-tuning could improve performance in challenging terrain
  • Data Augmentation Value: More diverse training data needed for edge cases like partial cloud cover and mixed disasters
