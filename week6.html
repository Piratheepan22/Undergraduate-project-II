Week 6

Overview

This week, I expanded our change detection dataset beyond RGB imagery to include comprehensive Multispectral Instrument (MSI) data from Sentinel-2 satellites. I developed a systematic workflow in Google Earth Engine to collect 13-band multispectral image triplets (before, during, after) for the same disaster-affected locations. This expansion significantly enhances our dataset's capability for detecting changes related to floods and landslides using spectral information beyond the visible spectrum.

Detailed Update

Why Expand to MSI?

RGB-only imagery has limitations for disaster detection. It cannot capture moisture content, struggles to differentiate land cover types, and is sensitive to lighting conditions. Sentinel-2 MSI provides 13 spectral bands from coastal aerosol to shortwave infrared (SWIR), enabling:

  • Better water detection: NIR and SWIR bands are strongly absorbed by water
  • Vegetation monitoring: Red edge bands detect vegetation stress and loss
  • Material discrimination: SWIR bands differentiate soil, rock, and urban materials
  • Spectral indices: NDVI, NDWI, and NBR for robust change detection

Sentinel-2 MSI Bands

The 13 bands span different spatial resolutions and spectral ranges:

  • 10m bands: Blue (B2), Green (B3), Red (B4), NIR (B8) - visible and near-infrared
  • 20m bands: Red Edge (B5-B7), Narrow NIR (B8A), SWIR (B11-B12) - vegetation and moisture
  • 60m bands: Coastal Aerosol (B1), Water Vapor (B9), Cirrus (B10) - atmospheric correction

Key bands for disaster detection:

  • B8 (NIR): Essential for NDVI and water detection
  • B5-B7 (Red Edge): Vegetation stress and health monitoring
  • B11-B12 (SWIR): Moisture content, flood mapping, landslide scars

GEE Workflow Overview

The automated workflow includes:

  1. Data Loading: Load COPERNICUS/S2_HARMONIZED and S2_CLOUD_PROBABILITY collections
  2. Temporal Windows: Define before (90 days), during (±3 days), after (10 days) periods
  3. Cloud Filtering: Join with cloud probability, rank by cloud fraction, select clearest image
  4. Band Selection: Extract all 13 bands, resample to 10m grid
  5. Export: Cloud-optimized GeoTIFF to Google Drive with standardized naming

Advantages Over RGB

  • NIR/SWIR capture moisture and vegetation signals invisible to RGB
  • Red edge bands detect subtle vegetation stress from disasters
  • SWIR bands enable accurate flood extent mapping
  • Spectral indices normalize lighting variations and shadows
  • Better material separation (soil vs. concrete vs. water)
