Overview

This week, we decided to focus our change detection dataset on the devastating Cyclone Ditwah disaster that struck Sri Lanka in late November 2025. we conducted extensive research to identify and document the affected areas, specifically focusing on the severe flooding in the Northern/North-Central/central provinces and the landslides in the Badulla district. Using Google Maps and disaster management reports, we compiled a comprehensive list of locations (ground truth points) for our dataset. Additionally, i continued reviewing research papers on change detection methodologies and dataset creation suitable for these specific terrains.

Detailed Update

Building on last week's foundation with Google Earth Engine, this week was dedicated to defining the specific focus of our change detection dataset. The November 2025 disaster presented a critical opportunity to create a meaningful dataset that could contribute to disaster management and future research in automated change detection systems.

The November 2025 Disaster (Cyclone Ditwah)

From November 26-29, 2025, heavy rainfall intensified by Cyclone Ditwah caused widespread devastation. Unlike typical monsoon rains, this event created distinct spectral signatures suitable for hyperspectral analysis due to the rapid sediment accumulation in water bodies and massive slope failures in the hill country.

Why This Dataset is Important

Creating a comprehensive change detection dataset for this specific disaster serves multiple purposes:

  • Spectral Diversity: Captures both water-based (flood) and soil-based (landslide) changes.
  • Ground Truth Validation: The distinct nature of the Madolsima landslide allows for high-confidence labeling.
  • Disaster Management: Supports rapid response models for future cyclones in the Bay of Bengal.
